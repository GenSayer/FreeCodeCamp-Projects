          <li><strong>1914</strong> - Born in Cresco, Iowa</li>
          <li><strong>1933</strong> - Left his family's farm to attend the University of Minnesota.</li>
          <li><strong>1937</strong> - Graduates with a degree in forestry from the University of Minnesota and begins studying plant pathology at the University of Minnesota Graduate School.</li>
          <li><strong>1944</strong> - Begins working with the Rockefeller Foundation in Mexico, focusing on wheat breeding and improving agricultural techniques to combat famine.</li>
          <li><strong>1945</strong> - Borlaugâ€™s wheat research in Mexico yields varieties that are resistant to rust diseases, increasing wheat production and laying the foundation for the Green Revolution.</li>
          <li><strong>1950s</strong> - The new wheat varieties are introduced to India and Pakistan, leading to substantial increases in wheat yields and helping to avert famine.</li>
          <li><strong>1968</strong> - Receives the International Maize and Wheat Improvement Center's (CIMMYT) award for his contributions to wheat and maize research.</li>
          <li><strong>1970</strong> - Awarded the Nobel Peace Prize for his contributions to increasing food production, which helped prevent famine and saved millions of lives.</li>
          <li><strong>1974</strong> - Establishes the Borlaug Institute for International Agriculture at Texas A&M University to continue his work on improving global food security.</li>
          <li><strong>1986</strong> - Receives the Congressional Gold Medal for his achievements in improving global food security.</li>
          <li><strong>2004</strong> - Dr. Borlaug is awarded the Presidential Medal of Freedom, the highest civilian award in the United States.</li>
          <li><strong>2009</strong> - Passes away on September 12 at the age of 95. He is widely remembered as the "Father of the Green Revolution" for his work in agricultural development.</li>
